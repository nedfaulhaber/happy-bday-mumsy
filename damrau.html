Diana Damrau

Die Zauberflöte, K. 620, Act 2: "Der Hölle Rache" (Königin der Nacht) composed by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, with Le Cercle de l'Harmonie, conducted by Jérémie Rhorer
from Arie Di Bravura (2008)

The first time that opera singer Diana Damrau came across my path, I was watching a video of her performing her signature role of the Queen of the Night in Wolgang Amadeus Mozart's Die Zauberflöte (The Magic Flute). The brilliant, light-speed, sky-high runs of melody coming forth from her mouth were unbelievable, literally. I wasn't quite sure what I was seeing and hearing - it seemed impossible to me that she could be singing like that, in perfect tune with the orchestra, at that speed and with that many pitches, and also moving around the stage acting out her role. The stunning costumery of the performance added to the mind-blowing experience, and I was compelled to seek some audio recordings out as soon as possible.
